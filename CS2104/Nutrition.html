A bean burrito is a popular Mexican-inspired dish consisting of a soft flour tortilla filled with seasoned refried beans, cheese, and spices. It is often garnished with toppings such as salsa, sour cream, or guacamole. Known for its rich, savory flavor and creamy texture, a bean burrito is a satisfying and versatile meal. From a nutritional perspective, it is a good source of protein and fiber, making it a filling option for vegetarians. On average, a standard bean burrito contains around 350-400 calories, with 12-15 grams of protein and 8-10 grams of fiber, depending on the ingredients. However, it can also be high in carbohydrates and sodium, especially if topped with cheese and sauces. When prepared with whole wheat tortillas and reduced-fat cheese, it can be a healthier, nutrient-dense meal.
